Physics Olympiad (For Iranians)

The Iranian National Physics Olympiad is a unique and intense journey. In this section, I share my advice for Iranian students navigating the stages—from the first round (مرحله اول) to the intensive summer camps (دوره چهل). Escaping into the world of physics problems was my version of fiction back then.

Preparing for the IPhO requires not just talent, but a very specific kind of discipline. It was through this process that I learned how to handle high-stakes environments and competitive pressure, something that later helped me navigate the PhD process at Harvard.

Recommended Books & Resources

  • Mechanics: Morin's Introduction to Classical Mechanics (The "Red Book").
  • Electromagnetism: Purcell & Morin.
  • Problem Solving: 200 Puzzling Physics Problems.

Applying to US Undergrad (For Everyone)

Applying to schools like MIT from abroad can feel like a complete mystery. Unlike most domestic systems, the US application is holistic—your grades matter, but so does your story, your curiosity, and your impact. For international students, especially those from the Middle East, understanding how to translate your local achievements into a format US admissions officers appreciate is key.
